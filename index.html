About me

I'm a senior algorithms researcher at Via Transportation, where I work on real-time route and shift planning. Before Via I spent three years as an algorithms researcher in the Israeli Air Force AI sector (Ofek 324), working on aerial navigation systems – a "Waze for aircraft" – with multi-objective path finding and TSP on random graphs.

My academic background is in computer science: I completed my B.Sc. at Bar-Ilan University ranked first among graduates, finished my M.Sc. with an AI specialization (grade 97), and I'm now a Ph.D. candidate. My M.Sc. thesis on multi-robot dynamic swarm disablement was published at IROS 2022.

Outside of algorithms work, I write Torah commentary – a hobby I've kept for over a decade. Two books are available on this site: Avi Ve'Yishi (2026) and Eli Teshukati (2020).
